# Lead Management Workflow

Effective lead management is the foundation of a consistent and scalable sales process. This document defines the complete lead pipeline for SyncBrand Media — from the moment an inquiry is received to the final outcome of winning or losing a deal. Each stage has a clear definition, prescribed sales actions, and explicit triggers that move the lead forward, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## Pipeline Overview

```
New Lead → Contacted → Interested → Proposal Sent → Negotiation → Won / Lost
```

---

## Stage-by-Stage Breakdown

### Stage 1: New Lead

**What it means:**
A new inquiry has entered the system. This could be from a website contact form, social media message, referral, cold outreach import, or a networking event. The lead has not yet been engaged by the sales team.

**Actions the sales team takes:**
- Review the lead details: name, company, contact info, project type, and budget (if provided)
- Score the lead based on predefined criteria (industry fit, budget range, urgency)
- Assign the lead to the appropriate sales executive (manual or auto-assignment)
- Add any initial notes or tags (e.g., "referred by XYZ," "high priority")

**Trigger to move to next stage:**
The sales executive makes the first outreach attempt — via phone call, email, or WhatsApp message. The lead is moved to **Contacted** as soon as communication is initiated, regardless of whether a response has been received.

---

### Stage 2: Contacted

**What it means:**
The sales team has made at least one outreach attempt. The lead is aware of SyncBrand's existence but has not yet expressed clear interest. The goal at this stage is to establish a connection and qualify the lead.

**Actions the sales team takes:**
- Make follow-up calls or send follow-up emails (at least 2–3 touchpoints over 5–7 days)
- Log every attempt with date, time, and outcome in the lead's activity timeline
- Ask discovery questions to understand the lead's pain points, goals, and timeline
- Share a brief company introduction or credentials deck if requested
- Set a reminder for the next follow-up if no response is received

**Trigger to move to next stage:**
The lead responds and expresses genuine interest in learning more about SyncBrand's services — either by asking questions, requesting a meeting, or sharing project details. The lead is moved to **Interested** upon confirmed engagement.

---

### Stage 3: Interested

**What it means:**
The lead has responded positively and is open to a deeper conversation. They are evaluating SyncBrand as a potential partner. This is the qualification and discovery stage.

**Actions the sales team takes:**
- Schedule and conduct a discovery call or meeting (in-person or virtual)
- Understand the full project scope: deliverables, timeline, audience, goals
- Identify the decision-maker and understand the approval process
- Assess budget range to determine commercial fit
- Share relevant portfolio work, case studies, or testimonials aligned with the lead's industry
- Internally assess feasibility and resource availability

**Trigger to move to next stage:**
The lead confirms interest in receiving a formal proposal and shares sufficient information (project brief, budget, timeline) for the team to prepare one. The lead is moved to **Proposal Sent** once the proposal document is dispatched.

---

### Stage 4: Proposal Sent

**What it means:**
A formal proposal or quotation has been sent to the lead. The proposal includes the scope of work, pricing breakdown, timeline, deliverables, and terms. The lead is actively reviewing the offer.

**Actions the sales team takes:**
- Send the proposal via the platform's document-sharing feature with a tracking link
- Follow up within 48 hours to confirm receipt and answer any initial questions
- Schedule a proposal walkthrough call if the lead has complex requirements
- Be available to clarify doubts about deliverables, pricing, or timelines
- Set a proposal validity deadline to create urgency (e.g., "Valid for 7 days")
- Log all responses and feedback on the proposal in the platform

**Trigger to move to next stage:**
The lead comes back with questions, requests changes to the proposal terms, or asks to discuss pricing. This indicates active consideration and signals the beginning of a negotiation. The lead is moved to **Negotiation**.

---

### Stage 5: Negotiation

**What it means:**
The lead is seriously considering moving forward but needs adjustments to scope, pricing, payment terms, or timelines before committing. This stage requires both commercial flexibility and clear boundary-setting.

**Actions the sales team takes:**
- Engage in structured discussions around pricing, scope adjustments, or milestone payment plans
- Involve the Admin or Business Head if discounts beyond a set threshold are requested
- Revise and resend the proposal if significant changes are agreed upon
- Clarify what is and isn't included in the revised scope to prevent future disputes
- Set a final decision deadline in writing (e.g., "Please confirm by [Date] to reserve the project slot")
- Maintain a record of all negotiation points in the platform's notes/activity log

**Trigger to move to next stage:**
The lead either formally accepts the proposal (moves to **Won**) or decides not to proceed at this time (moves to **Lost**). A verbal confirmation plus a signed agreement or advance payment triggers the Won status.

---

### Stage 6A: Won ✅

**What it means:**
The lead has signed the agreement and/or made an advance payment. They are now a confirmed client. The sales process is complete and the operations process begins.

**Actions upon winning:**
- Mark the lead as "Won" in the platform — triggers workflow automation
- Convert the lead record into a new Client profile
- Create a new Project linked to the client with agreed scope and timeline
- Assign the project to the relevant design/strategy team
- Send a branded welcome email to the client with onboarding details
- Issue the first invoice for the advance payment
- Schedule a kick-off meeting within the first 48 hours

---

### Stage 6B: Lost ❌

**What it means:**
The lead did not convert — either they chose a competitor, found the pricing unfit, had an internal decision change, or went silent. This is a valuable data point for improving future sales processes.

**Actions upon losing:**
- Mark the lead as "Lost" and select a reason from a predefined dropdown (e.g., "Price," "Competitor," "No Response," "Project Cancelled")
- Log a final note with any insights gained during the interaction
- Add the contact to a nurture list for future outreach (e.g., quarterly newsletter, milestone greetings)
- Analyze lost lead patterns monthly to identify gaps in the sales process or pricing strategy
- Do not delete the lead record — historical data is valuable for reporting

---

## How This Workflow Helps Track and Convert Leads

A structured lead pipeline transforms an ad hoc sales process into a predictable, measurable system. When every lead is assigned a stage, every interaction is logged, and every movement is triggered by a defined action — it becomes easy to:

- **Identify bottlenecks:** If most leads stall at "Proposal Sent," it may indicate a pricing or proposal quality issue.
- **Forecast revenue:** Knowing the number of leads at each stage (with their estimated deal values) allows the Admin to forecast monthly and quarterly revenue with reasonable accuracy.
- **Hold the team accountable:** Sales executives can be evaluated on stage-wise conversion rates, response times, and follow-up consistency.
- **Improve over time:** Lost lead reasons, stage durations, and conversion rates provide data to continuously refine the sales playbook.

### Practical Example

> **Scenario:** A startup founder fills out the contact form on syncbrandmedia.com requesting a complete brand identity package.
>
> 1. **New Lead:** The system auto-creates a lead record and scores it as "High Priority" (startup, branding package, urgent timeline). Auto-assigned to Sales Executive Priya.
> 2. **Contacted:** Priya calls within 2 hours. No answer. She logs the attempt and emails the founder the same evening.
> 3. **Interested:** The founder replies the next morning, shares a brief deck, and agrees to a discovery call on Thursday.
> 4. **Proposal Sent:** After the discovery call, Priya drafts a ₹75,000 brand identity proposal covering logo, brand guidelines, social media kit, and stationery. Sent on Friday with a 7-day validity.
> 5. **Negotiation:** The founder requests a 10% discount and asks to split payment into two milestones. Priya checks with the Admin and agrees to the milestone split but holds the price.
> 6. **Won:** The founder signs the agreement on Monday and transfers the 50% advance. Priya marks the lead as Won. The system automatically creates the project, assigns the design team, and sends the client a welcome email — all within seconds.

This end-to-end workflow ensures that no lead is forgotten, every interaction is documented, and the transition from prospect to active client is seamless and professional.
